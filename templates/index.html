  • Early Beginnings (2014-2016).
  • Mide's Wood was established in 2014 by [Kikelomo Jayeola], with a vision to provide high-quality wood products to builders, craftsmen, and homeowners. The business started in a modest warehouse, focusing on sourcing and supplying imported furniture woods. Initially, Mide's Wood served small contractors and DIY enthusiasts, offering a selection of hardwoods and softwoods known for their durability and natural beauty..
  • Expansion and Growth (2017-2019)

In the early 2020s, Mide's Wood focused on establishing itself as a leader in sustainable and premium wood products. The company launched an online store, making it easier for customers to browse and order wood products from anywhere. Despite the challenges brought by the COVID-19 pandemic, Mide's Wood adapted quickly, offering curbside pickup and delivery services, which kept the business.
Today, Mide's Wood. is a trusted name in the furniture industry, known for its commitment to quality, sustainability, and customer service. The company continues to innovate, offering a wide range of wood products, from wooden doors to fine imported furniture woods, and has become a go-to resource for those seeking the best in wood materials.

MDF, or Medium-Density Fiberboard, is an engineered wood product made from wood fibers, wax, and resin that are compressed under high heat and pressure. Here are some key points about MDF:.

Smooth Surface: MDF has a very smooth surface, making it ideal for painting and veneering. It’s often used for cabinets, furniture, and decorative moldings.

  • Cost-Effective: Generally more affordable than solid wood or plywood, MDF provides a budget-friendly option for many woodworking projects.
  • Versatile: It can be easily cut, shaped, and drilled, which makes it a popular choice for custom furniture and intricate designs..
  • No Knots or Grain: Unlike solid wood, MDF doesn’t have knots or a grain pattern, allowing for a uniform appearance..

Susceptibility to Moisture: MDF can swell or warp if exposed to excessive moisture, so it's not ideal for high-humidity areas unless it’s specially treated. Environmental Impact: Many MDF products are made from recycled wood fibers, making it a more sustainable choice compared to solid wood.
